Love Scraps

[jas]

2020/02/18

On whim of bless'd sun
bring green fields through season's thrum
pass your time with fletcher and sparrow
bracken wood old and narrow
Deep into ancient dell
deeper into nature's spell
Her strands of life hanging down
flowing into mossy gown
Commune with nymph, babbling brook bound
Listen to its siren sound
Earthen perfume allure
to rolling curves demure
Inviting and exciting
unseen sights delighting
Secret since days of yore
and all you want is more

There's nothing like a love furnished with all accoutrement of a
mature soul, wise in word, you'll be as wealthy as Crassus.
Though belated it was innocent as youth, immature too.
Green and free as that once, potential manifesting but little more
than memory now. What once could have been, what once would be.
Future open as a cloudless sky, though darker now.

Honey in my mind, her sweet words were kind
Now Summer's sun is setting on our loves begetting
The time was too short to learn the ways of her heart
but for the Septembers spent
with arms bent
around her shoulder
on cracking concrete bench
in trees turning color
cold creeping closer
October already over and you're her lover
And you'll love her, 'til November

A mind that multiplies the smallest matters
sits around candle light in the darkest hours
his sight, though shaded by the world glows
from snowed in cabin he knew then
a thought sent from outer heaven

Never worry about the parts of yourself which clatteringly scatter
across moonlit moss
Make some sense of the burden of love's essence
Burdensome, but above all
rise again that you may fall
sinking into the night, call
upon the faerie fawn
nipping at that moss
as through and into the woods you've lost

your way
