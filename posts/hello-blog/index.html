In the Beginning*

[Jacob A. Sonnenberg]

2019/10/20

This is the first real post that I intend to publish on a blog that should have been, at least since yesteryear. Alas, a lack of motivation or discipline for seeing through the smaller things obscures The Way.

Let it be, allow the things you see to lead. The words will weave the way if you don’t forget to speak.

Yet, you see. It’s nothing new to you or me! Echoes of words and their meanings meandering through open ears, open minds. Perhaps provoking primordial understanding, that would be The Way, fetterless, free.

I’m not one to bear the burden of a over-heavy work-flow though, if it should take much work to spew my words into the void I likely won’t. So the work-flow is vitally important! Moving past the flowery words of self-aggrandizement!

With a combination of Emacs’ org-capture, plus the ox-hugo and easy-hugo interfaces for the lovely static site generator hugo and a stray deploy.sh script I’ve got the publishing work-flow down to

C-c c b
C-c C-e H H
C-c b
G

Where C-c c b is my capture template for a blog post, captured to ~/org/blog.org.

C-c C-e H H exports the sub-tree as markdown to the hugo directory.

C-c b calls easy-hugo for a comprehensive Emacs interface.

And from there G runs deploy.sh and publishes the new post to github pages.

And so the spell is cast! With a common invocation of ten (chorded) keystrokes.
